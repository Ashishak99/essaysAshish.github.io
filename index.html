Essay on Diwali in English

Lord Ganesha and Mother Lakshmi are worshipped on Deepawali. This festival shows the victory of light over darkness. On this day, lamps are lit in homes. Different types of lights, colourful lights are installed. People wear new clothes. Sweets are distributed in the evening, and people go to feasts. When is 2020 Diwali? This festival will be celebrated on 14 November 2020. History of Diwali Deepawali is one of the major festivals celebrated in India. This festival is celebrated by people of Sikh, Buddhist, and Jainism. The Sikh community celebrates this as a captive leave day. On the day of Diwali, Lord Shri Ram returned to Ayodhya after deducting 14 years of exile. The residents of Ayodhya lit ghee lamps to welcome Sri Ram. That day was the new moon of Kartik month. The people of Ayodhya lit lamps to light in the dark darkness. Since then, every day is celebrated by all Indians as Prakash Parv (Deepawali). This festival shows that good always triumphs over evil, truth triumphs forever. This festival is celebrated on the new moon day of Kartik month. On this day, Lord Rama returned to Ayodhya after cutting 14 years of exile with Sita Mata. This festival is celebrated in the same happiness. On this day, the Pandavas returned home after 12 years of exile. According to the Hindi calendar, the new year begins on the day of Deepawali. This festival shows the victory of good over evil, light over darkness, and knowledge over ignorance. Origin of the word ‘Deepawali.’ The word Deepavali has its origin in 2 Sanskrit words ‘Deep’ and ‘Awali.’ Deep means light while avali means line. In this way, Deepawali means the line of lights. How is the celebration of Diwali celebrated? All children and elders wait for the Diwali festival. People clean their houses. They pick up all kinds of garbage and throw it out. After that, the walls, houses get colored. Shopkeepers sweep their shop and do new paintings. All markets shine as soon as Deepawali arrives. Are filled with goods. Children and men celebrate their happiness with firecrackers. All people worship Ganesha, Lakshmi, and other deities with full law. Goddess Lakshmi is requested to come home, which brings happiness to the house. Importance of Diwali On this day, thousands of people buy gold ornaments, utensils, clothes, and other things. The festival is a staple for shopkeepers and traders as sales are highest on this day. They get a chance to make more and more profit. All shopkeepers eagerly wait for this day. Traders of firecrackers and fireworks stuff make a lot of profit by selling goods on this day. Crackers also cause air and noise pollution. The festival of Dhanteras takes place two days before Deepawali. On this day, the markets are very bright. It is considered auspicious to make utensils, gold ornaments, TV, fridge, cooler, washing machine on Dhanteras. People bring idols of Kheel Batashe and Ganesh Lakshmi to worship in the evening. On the day of Deepawali, people believe that by worshiping Ganesh Lakshmi, she will come into the house. We all need Lakshmi (money). Therefore, this festival becomes even more critical. Worshiping Ganesha Lakshmi increases wealth and happiness. Affluence comes at home. On this day, the god of wealth “Kubera” is also worshiped. Bhai Dooj and Deepawali On the day of Bhai Dooj, brothers and sisters combine and bathe in the river Yamuna. The sister puts a tilak on the brother’s forehead and wishes that he always be away from sorrows and sorrows. The brother gives his sister money or any other gift. Diwali festival abroad This festival is celebrated in India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, Mauritius, Singapore, Pakistan-Australia. In Nepal, it is celebrated with special pomp. On this day, the new year begins in the Nepali era. The festival of Diwali in Nepal- lasts for five days. The crows are worshiped on the first day. He is offered, Prasad. On the second day, the dog is offered for his honesty. On the third day, offerings are made to the cow. Prasad is offered to the bull on the fourth day. On Diwali in Malaysia, people of other religions are feasted at home. The people of the Tamil sect celebrate Deepavali in Sri Lanka. On this day, dances, feasts, and fireworks are performed.
